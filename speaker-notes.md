# Speaker Notes — Ntegra Product Engineering OKRs
*Estimated session time: 60–75 minutes including workshop*

---

## Slide 1 — Cover

Welcome everyone. Thank you for being here. Today is about something bigger than a new planning process — it's about changing how Product Engineering thinks about success. We're going to cover our mission, introduce OKRs properly, and then you'll have the chance to help build our first set together. I'd ask you to hold questions until the end of each section — we'll have plenty of time for discussion, and the workshop at the end is where the real thinking happens.

*Timing: 1–2 minutes*

---

## Slide 2 — The Real Reason

Let's be direct with ourselves. If I told you we're introducing a new goal-setting framework, you'd be forgiven for thinking: here we go again. But that's not what this is. OKRs aren't a process change. They're a thinking change. What we celebrate, what we call success, what we put in front of leadership — all of that shifts. We stop celebrating effort and start celebrating impact. That is a meaningful change, and I want you to feel the weight of it.

*Timing: 2–3 minutes*

---

## Slide 3 — Why Change?

These three observations are honest reflections of where we are right now. They are not criticisms — they describe challenges that affect most engineering organisations of our size and ambition. Naming them is the first step. Career visibility: engineers routinely do exceptional work that never gets connected to their growth story. Ownership: we have real variation across teams in how much decision-making authority engineers experience. Routine work: our most talented people are spending meaningful time on work that doesn't require their level of skill. Every one of these is an opportunity. That's why we're here.

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 4 — The Future

Close your eyes for a moment — not literally, you'll miss the slides — but imagine it. One year from now: every engineer in Product Engineering has a documented development plan and genuinely understands how they can grow. Teams are making decisions, not just receiving them. And we're spending more of our collective talent on the work that defines us — the hard, creative, high-value problems. That's the destination. OKRs are how we navigate to it.

*Timing: 2–3 minutes*

---

## Slide 5 — Mission

This is our north star. Read it with me: "Make Product Engineering a destination for exceptional people to join, grow and stay." The three words matter: join, grow, and stay. We want to attract great people. We want to develop them. And we want them to choose to remain. Every decision we make in Product Engineering should trace back to this mission. When we're debating priorities, when we're making hiring decisions, when we're choosing what to invest in — this is the question: does this make us a better destination for exceptional people?

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 6 — Three Strategic Pillars

Three pillars flow directly from the mission. Clear Career Pathways — the "grow" part. Meaningful Ownership — the part that makes the work itself rewarding. Innovation and High-Value Work — the part that makes us the kind of place exceptional people want to be. Every initiative, every project, every investment we make should strengthen at least one of these pillars. If it doesn't, we should genuinely question whether it's the right use of our capacity. These pillars are our filter.

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 7 — Why OKRs?

Traditional project management asks a binary question: did we deliver? OKRs ask a different, more important question: did anything change? In the old model, a project ends and we declare victory. But nobody asks: did anyone's career actually advance? Did our engineers feel more ownership? Did we move the needle on anything that matters? OKRs create a continuous loop — we set an intention, we measure the result, we learn, we improve. That loop is what makes organisations get better over time.

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 8 — The Strategy Cascade

Think of this as a waterfall of purpose. The Mission never changes — it's who we are. Objectives are our quarterly bets — the meaningful changes we believe we can make in three months. Key Results are the evidence that we've achieved the objective. Not the activity — the evidence. And Initiatives are the specific work we choose to run. Most teams operate at the initiative layer and stop there. They never look up and ask: is this work actually moving anything? OKRs force that question.

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 9 — Anatomy of an OKR

Let's make this concrete with a real example. The Objective is qualitative: "Create clear career pathways across Product Engineering." It's inspiring. It's directional. You know when you read it whether it matters. The Key Results are what make it real: publish a framework, 75% of colleagues with documented development plans, 90% understanding their progression opportunities. Each Key Result is specific, measurable, and time-bound. The Initiatives — framework design, mentoring, coaching, workshops — are the work. But the Initiatives alone don't prove success. Only the Key Results can do that.

*Timing: 4–5 minutes*

---

## Slide 10 — The Most Common Mistake

This is where most OKR implementations fail, and I want to be very clear about it. Activities are things you do. Outcomes are things that change. "Launch a mentoring programme" is an activity — I can do it in a week and it tells you nothing about whether anyone benefited. "80% of colleagues have an active mentor" is an outcome — it tells you something real happened. The discipline of OKRs is always asking: so what changed? Always pushing from the left column to the right. If your Key Result could be ticked off on day one by running a meeting, it's an activity, not an outcome.

*Timing: 4–5 minutes*

---

## Slide 11 — Our First Objectives

These three objectives are our first bets. They map one-to-one with our three pillars. Objective 1 is about career pathways and development — the "grow" pillar. Objective 2 is about meaningful ownership — the part of our mission that makes work rewarding. Objective 3 is about ensuring our engineering capacity is focused on the right things. These are ambitious but achievable within a quarter with focused effort. In the workshop, you'll help define the Key Results that make each of these measurable.

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 12 — Dashboard

This is what our end-of-quarter review could look like. Not a status report — a learning tool. Each metric tells a story. Career framework coverage at 85%: we published the framework, most roles are covered, a few gaps remain. Development plans at 75%: better than nothing, but we haven't reached everyone. Understanding of progression at 90%: our communication worked. These numbers don't judge us — they direct our attention. Where do we need to focus next quarter? The dashboard exists to trigger the right conversations.

*Note: Numbers shown are illustrative targets — actual Key Results to be agreed in workshop.*

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 13 — Quarterly Rhythm

OKRs aren't a set-and-forget exercise. The planning at the start of the quarter is only the beginning. The real value is in the rhythm — the regular check-ins where we ask honestly: what's working? What's not? Do we need to adjust? I'd suggest setting a recurring 30-minute team check-in every two weeks. Make it a habit. Bring your Key Results. Talk about what's moving and what's stuck. Learn as you go. OKRs get better every quarter precisely because you treat them as a learning system, not an accountability mechanism.

*Timing: 3–4 minutes*

---

## Slide 14 — Workshop

Now it's your turn. We're going to spend the next 20–25 minutes doing the real work. Break into groups of three or four — I'll assign groups in a moment. Each group takes one of our three objectives. Follow the four steps on the left: choose the objective, draft three Key Results, identify initiatives, then challenge and refine together. Use the quality questions on the right as your filter. Would success be obvious? Can it be measured? Does it connect to our mission? Are we measuring outcomes not effort? Someone in each group, please volunteer to write things down — we'll share back at the end.

*Timing: Workshop = 20–25 minutes + 10 minutes sharing back = 30–35 minutes total*

---

## Slide 15 — Closing

Thank you. Three truths to take with you. Objectives inspire — they give us a direction worth moving toward. Key Results measure — they tell us, honestly, whether we moved. Initiatives deliver — they're the work we choose to do in service of what matters. If you remember nothing else today, remember this: measure what matters, and the right work follows. When we measure the right things, we stop optimising for activity and start optimising for impact. That's the promise of OKRs. That's the promise we're making to each other today.

Any final questions?

*Timing: 2–3 minutes*
